Hack Camp is a two-week summer program for high school students who want to make things with code. We'll bring you from your first line of code to publishing your first project.

When is Hack Camp?

Hack Camp sessions start every two weeks, beginning June 1st (e.g. June 1st, June 15th, June 29th). Applications are currently being accepted on a rolling basis.

Where is Hack Camp?

Hack Camp will take place at Galvanize SF in downtown San Francisco in the heart of the city's technology scene. It is an 8 minute walk from Montgomery St. BART and Muni Metro station .

When are applications due?

We're doing admissions on a rolling basis, so if you've already applied and haven't heard back from us yet, don't worry! You'll hear back from us soon.

Who Hack Camp for?

Hack Camp is for high school students who want to create apps, build websites, and make games. Over two exhilarating weeks, you'll learn fundamental web development skills transferable to game and app development. We welcome girls and boys ages 14-18, no prior experience necessary. Young adults with some experience are welcome as well, of course. Please bring your own laptop.

How much does Hack Camp cost?

Hack Camp costs $595 for the two-week program. However, inclusivity is extremely important to us and we are readily offering full need-based financial aid (see the question on the application form).

Who's mentoring this?

Hack Camp is led by the co-founders of hackEDU, two wonderfully optimistic and energetic guys: Zach Latta and Jonathan Leung. With 15 years of coding experience between them, they are a stellar team. Zach is an accomplished programmer, with multiple applications in the App Store with over 4 million users. Jonathan is an experienced coder and curriculum writer: he grew the now prestigious PennApps hackathon from 80 to 1000 participants and taught at All Star Code so effectively that his students went on to form their own coding non-profit.

FAQ

Do you provide housing?

We really wish we could accommodate out-of-state students with housing, but unfortunately won't be able this first year.

What do I need to bring?

You should bring your laptop, lunch, and a thirst for knowledge. Let us know if you don't have a laptop, and we'll do our best to accomodate you.

How many other students will be there?

Each session will vary in size between 4 and 12 campers. The student-mentor ratio will never exceed 4:1. For session specifics, please contact us at summer@hackedu.us .

I don't know how to code!

Perfect! Hack Camp was built with beginners in mind.
You'll be up to speed in no time.

What if I already know how to code?

There's always more to learn—why not surround yourself with awesome makers and doers with similar interests?

What time of day is camp?

Camp is Monday-Friday from 9:20pm to 4:00pm. Each camp goes for 2 weeks.

What about transportation?

Students are responsible for their own transportation to and from Galvanize SF (Clipper cards are highly recommended).

What will students do aside from code?

Students will participate in team bonding activities and more!

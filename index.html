Hack Camp is a two-week summer program for high school students who want to make things with code. We'll bring you from your first line of code to publishing your first project.

When is Hack Camp?

Hack Camp is two weeks long, with multiple sessions throughout the summer. Sessions start between June 1st and August 10th (e.g. June 1st, June 15th, June 29th, etc.).

Where is Hack Camp?

Hack Camp will take place in downtown San Francisco in the heart of the city's technology scene. Exact location will be sent to applicants upon acceptance.

When are applications due?

First round of applications due Saturday, May 23rd at 12pm PDT.

Who Hack Camp for?

Hack Camp is for high school students who want to create apps, build websites, and make games. Over two exhilerating weeks, you'll learn fundamental skills transferable to game and app development. We welcome girls and boys ages 14-18, no prior experience necessary. Young adults with some experience are welcome as well, of course. Please bring your own laptop.

How much does Hack Camp cost?

Hack Camp costs $595 for the two-week program. However, inclusivity is extremely important to us and we are readily offering full need-based financial aid (see the question on the application form).

How many other students will be there?

Each session will vary in size between 4 and 12 campers. The student-mentor ratio will never exceed 4:1. For the specifics for a session, please contact us at summer@hackedu.us.

I don't know how to code!

Perfect! Hack Camp was built with beginners in mind. You'll be up to speed in no time.

Who's mentoring this?

Hack Camp is led by the founders of hackEDU, Zach Latta and Jonathan Leung, with a combined 15 years of coding experience. Zach has multiple applications in the App Store with over 4 million users. Jonathan grew his college hackathon from 80 to 1,000+ participants and created curriculum at All Star Code.

FAQ

What do I need to bring?

You should bring your laptop, lunch, and a thirst for knowledge. Let us know if you don't have a laptop, and we'll do our best to accomodate you.

What if I already know how to code?

There's always more to learn—why not surround yourself with awesome makers and doers with similar interests?

What will students do aside from code?

Students will participate in team bonding activities, tech company tours, and more!
